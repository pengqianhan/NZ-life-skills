# Kiwi Access Card Current Policy

As of: `2026-04-17`

Use this file as the baseline policy summary for the `kiwi-access-card` skill. Before answering any question about current rules, fees, eligibility, or application steps, re-check the official sources below. If anything has changed, update this file.

## Baseline Policy Summary

### General Eligibility

- Kiwi Access Card is for people over 18, or within one month of turning 18.
- Applicants must be in New Zealand to apply.
- Official pages state the card is available to NZ citizens, NZ residents, foreign nationals, and visitors from overseas.
- Official traveller guidance states that people travelling, working, or studying in New Zealand can apply, including foreign students and other visitors to NZ.

### General Identity Documents

- The FAQ states that acceptable photo ID includes `Passport (NZ or overseas)`, NZ Driver Licence, and some additional documents.
- This means overseas passport holders, including Chinese passport holders, are generally eligible to apply.

### Online Application

- The current public online portal page lists these documents for completing an online application:
  - current New Zealand Drivers Licence
  - current New Zealand Passport, or one less than 2 years expired
  - New Zealand Birth Certificate
- The online portal also requires:
  - a working webcam or phone camera
  - a valid credit or debit card
  - email
  - a New Zealand delivery address
- The portal therefore appears narrower than the general eligibility pages. A person may be eligible to apply overall, but still be unable to complete the current online flow with an overseas passport.
- If the user is an international student with a foreign passport and the portal still lists only NZ-issued documents, advise that in-person application is the reliable path unless a live official source clearly says otherwise.

### In-Person Application

- In-person applications can be made at participating AA Centres, AA Driver Licensing Agents, or NZ Post shops, depending on the official page used.
- For in-person application, the user generally needs:
  - completed application form
  - two identical recent passport-sized colour photos
  - proof of New Zealand delivery address
  - original acceptable ID
  - payment

### Fees And Timing

- Online application fee: `NZ$70`
- In-person application fee: `NZ$75`
- Standard processing time: up to `20 working days`
- Seasonal delays may be longer in peak periods.

### Photo Rules

- Photos must be recent, colour, passport-sized, and compliant with the posted photo requirements.
- No selfies.
- The paper form workflow still expects physical printed photos for in-person submission.

### Name Change Limitation

- The online portal states that name changes cannot be processed online and should use the paper version of the application.

## Interpretation Notes

- Distinguish carefully between `eligible in principle` and `supported by the current online portal`.
- When a user asks about Chinese passport holders or international students, the safest wording is:
  - they appear eligible to apply in general under the official policy for foreign nationals / visitors / students
  - but the current online portal may not support overseas passports directly
  - in-person application is the dependable fallback unless the latest official online guidance changes
